our winery

Located in the heart of Sonoma County

How our wines are made

At Francis Ford Coppola Winery, our high quality wines require handpicking to keep out as many leaves as possible. Our grapes are then crushed, which simulates traditional foot stomping. The crushed grapes will then be piped off into massive tanks where fermentation takes place. Finally, the wine is kept in toasted oak barrels for aging, which will then be bottled.

Tours

Our tours at Francis Ford Coppola Winery include seeing each part of the winemaking process, wine tasting, tours of the property and movie gallery.

Our history

1920s

Agostino Coppola creates his first batch of wine

While growing up, Francis Ford Coppola heard stories at the dinner table of his grandfather, Agostino Coppola, and his seven sons making wine in the basement of their New York apartment building using concrete vats he constructed himself. These childhood stories are what sparked the idea of winemaking later in Francis’ life.

1920s
1975

Francis Ford Coppola purchases the Inglenook Estate in Napa Valley

After success of the films, such as “The Godfather”, Francis Ford Coppola purchases the Inglenook Estate in Napa Valley. Coppola was drawn to the rich history and winemaking heritage of the Inglenook Estate. He intended to restore the estate to its former glory and create world-class wines.

1975
2006

Francis Ford Coppola expands his winery to Sonoma County

From the success of Inglenook, Francis Ford Coppola expands his wine endeavors to a new estate located in Geyserville, Sonoma County. This is where the Francis Ford Coppola Winery would be founded.

2006
2010 – Present

The Francis Ford Coppola Winery officially opens

The Francis Ford Coppola Winery offers visitors an experience that is beyond winemaking. These include wine tasting, tours of the property, movie gallery, and vineyards, a rustic restaurant, event hosting, and activities for the whole family such as swimming pools and a park.
